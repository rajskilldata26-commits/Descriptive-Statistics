Q1: Understanding Central Tendency (Easy)
A bakery tracks the daily sales of muffins (in dozens) over a week: [10, 12, 11, 15, 14, 13, 12].
What is the most representative value of their weekly sales, and why?
SOL:-
Data: 10, 12, 11, 15, 14, 13, 12
1. Mean (Average)
Mean=10+12+11+15+14+13+12/7=87\7≈12.43
2. Median (Middle Value)
Sorted data: 10, 11, 12, 12, 13, 14, 15
Median = 12
3.Mode (Most Frequent Value)
Mode = 12 (appears twice)
Most Representative Value: 12 dozens
Why?
The median (12) is right in the middle of the data.
The mode (12) is the most common value.
The mean (~12.43) is very close to 12.

Q4: Median in Real Life (Medium)
A car dealer notes the prices of used cars: [$8,000, $9,500, $10,200, $11,000, $50,000].
Why is the median a better measure than the mean in this case? Calculate the median.
SOL:-
Prices: 8,000; 9,500; 10,200; 11,000; 50,000
Median (Middle Value)
The data is already in order:
8,000, 9,500, 10,200, 11,000, 50,000
Median: $10,200
Why median is better than mean here:
The value $50,000 is an extreme outlier (much higher than the rest).
If we calculate the mean, this one high price would pull the average up, making it seem like cars
are more expensive than they usually are.
The median is not affected by extreme values, so it better represents a typical car price.
Q5: Dispersion Introduction (Medium)
A student times how long it takes to finish a puzzle each day: [25, 30, 27, 35, 40].
What does the range tell us about the variation in the student’s puzzle-solving time?
SOL:-
Times: 25, 30, 27, 35, 40
Range (Measure of Dispersion)
Range=Maximum−Minimum=40−25=15
Range: 15 minutes
What this tells us:
The student’s puzzle-solving time varies by 15 minutes from the fastest to the slowest day.
This shows there is moderate variation in performance.
Some days the student is much quicker (25 min), while on others it takes longer (40 min).

Q7: Variance for Decision-Making (Medium)
Two delivery companies track delivery delays (in minutes).
Company A: variance = 6
Company B: variance = 15
Which company is more consistent, and why?
More Consistent Company: Company A
Why?
SOL:-
Variance measures how spread out the data is from the average.
A smaller variance means the values are closer to the mean → more consistent performance.
A larger variance means more fluctuation → less consistency.
Comparison:
Company A: variance = 6 (less spread)
Company B: variance = 15 (more spread)

Q8: Standard Deviation in Context (Hard)
A finance student compares the daily price fluctuations of two cryptocurrencies.
Coin A: standard deviation = $30
Coin B: standard deviation = $120
Which coin is riskier to invest in, and why?
SOL:-
Riskier Investment: Coin B
Why?
Standard deviation measures how much prices fluctuate around the average.
A higher standard deviation means prices change more dramatically → higher volatility →
higher risk.
Comparison:
Coin A: $30 (smaller fluctuations)
Coin B: $120 (much larger fluctuations)
Conclusion:
Coin B is riskier because its price varies much more from day to day. This means:
Potential for higher gains, but also
Greater chance of big losses
So, Coin B is less stable and more unpredictable compared to Coin A.
Q9: Combining Measures (Hard) A family records their monthly electricity usage (in kWh): [400,
420, 390, 450, 410].
Find the mean and standard deviation. What do these values together tell you about the family’s
energy use pattern?
SOL:-
Data: 400, 420, 390, 450, 410
1. Mean (Average)
Mean=400+420+390+450+410/5=2070\5=414
2. Standard Deviation
Step-by-step idea:
Find deviations from mean (414):
-14, +6, -24, +36, -4
Square them:
196, 36, 576, 1296, 16
Average of squares (variance):
2120/5=424
Standard deviation:
424≈20.6
Results:
Mean = 414 kWh
Standard deviation ≈ 20.6 kWh
What this tells us:
The family typically uses about 414 kWh per month.
The standard deviation (~20.6) is relatively small compared to the mean, meaning:
Their electricity usage is fairly consistent month to month.
There are some fluctuations, but not extreme ones.
